Notice of Year 2022/2023 AGMM

 

Glory be to God in the highest, may his name be exalted forever more. Amen

In line with the Constitution of the Foursquare Gospel Church in Nigeria, I hereby notify you (the church membership) that the 2022/2023 church year ends
June 30th, 2023 and that a New Foursquare year starts 1st July, 2023.
In line with the foregoing therefore, that the Annual General Membership
Meeting (AGMM) has been slated for Sunday 30th July, 2023 at the Church
Auditorium, shortly after Worship Service.

 

SPECIAL NOTE

1.0 The AGMM Report & Account soft copy will be sent to every card-carrying

member at least a day to the meeting for perusal and note taking.

2.0 No report shall be taken on the floor of the AGMM aside those of the Senior

Pastor, the Church Secretary and Treasurer. Others shall be taken as read and

adopted for discussion alongside the trios. This is essential to reduce time

consumption.

3.0 Fresh matters and other sundry issues/general shall be entertained on the floor

of the AGMM

4.0 COUNCIL ELECTION: There will be Council Election on the floor of the

AGMM
The following candidates have been nominated by the church council.

1. Bro. Bamidele Wilson (Re election)
2. Sis. Mary Akinbowale
3.  Bro. Idowu Bisade
4. Bro. Mike Esukpa

Two candidates with the highest number of votes will be returning to the
Council in the new year. The senior Pastor of the church is available for any comments.

Thanks very much, may God richly bless you as you pray along for a smooth and
successful AGMM.

DCN. ZUBAIR SAMUEL
Church Secretary

THE MINUTES OF THE ANNUAL GENERAL MEMBERSHIP MEETING OF THE FOURSQUARE GOSPEL CHURCH BIOYIN. HELD ON SUNDAY, JULY 31ST, 2022 IN THE CHURCH AUDITORIUM.

ATTENDANCE: In attendance were:

The Senior Pastor;

The Church Council Members;

The Pastorate;

74 Card carrying Members and 21 observers.

 

COMMENCEMENT

The meeting commenced at 12:20am with prayers and a session of worship led by Sis. Akinbowale Mary. Sis. Senami Hunpe led a section of praise and worship.

ACCREDITATION

The accreditation of members present was done by the Church Secretary, Dcn. Zubair Samuel. He also emphasized on the importance of worshippers to become card carrying members of Foursquare Gospel Church.

WELCOME ADDRESS

The Senior Pastor and the Chairman, Rev. Olaolu Oladeinde welcomed all the brethren into the meeting and also thanked God for the Foursquare year. He emphasized on the importance of having the AGMM. He added that members should be open and cautioned in their suggestions.

EXHORTATION

The word of exhortation was taken by Rev. Oladeinde from the book of Malachai chapter One. He stressed that as workers and leaders in the Vineyard of God, we must be responsible and accountable for the assignments committed to our hands. He added that it is not a good thing for us as leaders or workers to apportion blames where we are supposed to be responsible. He rounded off with words of prayers taken by Pst. Olusegun Oguntade.

 

READING AND ADOPTION OF LAST MINUTES

The Minutes of the last Annual General Membership Meeting was read by the Secretary, Dcn. Zubair Samuel, while the following observation was made on the minutes:

  Bro. Dipo Olorunda observed that there is a difference in the figure of members accredited and the attendance of the meeting.

In the absence of further corrections, the motion for the adoption of the minutes was raised by Mummy Adenike Ayeni and seconded by Sis. Janet Fasakin.

MATTER ARISING FROM THE MINUTE

  Sis. Olorunda: Appreciated the church leadership on the action taken to fix the fans. She added that the remaining should still be considered for fixing immediately to avoid further damage.

  Bro. Atanda: Observed the bad condition of the children drum set and their Television set.

  Bro. Akinbowale: Reminded the church of the welfare box at the back of the auditorium.

  Sis. Ayeni: Observed that members were not following the Secretarys report due to the method of presentation of the report, that is, using soft copy instead of the usual printed hard copy.

  Dcn. Sowunmi: Also, added that the soft copy method is not really encouraging. He further advised that church should find way to get fund so as to make hard copy available subsequently.

  Bro. Esukpa: Suggested that the hard copy of the report should be given to the members earlier before the meeting for better comprehension.

RESPONSE TO OBSEVATIONS RAISED: Rev. Oladeinde, the chairman of the Church Council responded to all the points raised and promised that actions will be taken on the observations in due time.

 

PRESENTATION OF REPORTS

A.    SENIOR PASTORS REPORT: The report was read by the Senior Pastor and the Chairman, Rev. Olaolu Oladeinde. In his presentation, he appreciated all leaders, members, workers and the entire church worshippers for the show of love and support for the church during the year under review. He prayed that the Lord Almighty shall reward each and everyone of us accordingly in Jesus name.

B.    THE CHURCH SECRETARY REPORT: The Secretary report was taken by the church secretary Dcn. Zubair Samuel. In his presentation, he appreciated God Almighty and thanked all members for the supports he received as the Church Secretary. He prayed that God will continue to move His church forward in Jesus name.

C.    THE CHURCH TREASURERS REPORT: Sis. Janet Atanda, the Church Treasurer presented the financial reports for the year under review. In her presentation, she thanked God for a successful year and pray that our labour of love in the vineyard of God will not be in vain in Jesus name. Though there was a little challenge presenting the report from the soft copy. She promised that the method of presentation will surely be improved on.

D.    MINISTRIES & DEPARTMENTAL REPORTS: The Arms and various Departments reports were taken as being read as the senior pastor commended each Arm or Department for jobs well done. However, the following observations were noted.

i.      Home and Life:

  Sis. Mary Akinbowale suggested that rather than having many House Fellowship centres not functioning, the centres be collapsed to few ones that are viable and functioning for effectiveness.

ii.      Children Ministry:

  Sis. Eluwade pointed out that there has not been any proper planning for the childrens camp. And as such, participation in this years Axis Childrens Camping Exercise is not certain.

  Bro. Oluranti quarry the security in place at the camp by the organizers of the camping in respect of the prevailing security challenges in the country.

  Dcns. Adebiyi urged the church leadership to be caution of security before allowing children to go on such journey.

  Pst. Oguntade also added his voice on the security issue with a scenario he heard of around the camp ground.

  In response, Rev. Oladeinde seek the membership opinion on either our children should be allowed to participate in the camping exercise or not. It was unanimously agreed that we observed the event this year and thereafter plan towards next year.

iii.      Drama Ministry:

  The Senior Pastor urged the Drama Team to be up and doing in the new Foursquare Year.

iv.      Maintenance Department:

  Sis. Naomi: Pointed out that despite complains made on different occasions, the condition of the toilet drainages remains the same.

  Bro. Maxson: Promised to look into the complains about the toilets.

v.      Youth Ministry:

  The Senior Pastor commended the Team for a job well done during the year under review. He quickly noted that the youth has always promised to support the teenagers but always fail.

  Pst. Oguntade also urged the youth to take up their responsibilities to church very important and work together as a team to achieve their aims.

  Mummy Ayeni also observe on the zero balance on the youth financial report is as a result of them having viable treasure who will run at them to collect money.

  Dcn. Sowunmi urged them to cultivate the habit of giving as this will help them grow financially.

  Rev. Oladeinde urged the youth to create rooms for regular fellowship and ensure there are more evangelistic programmes for the NetxGen.

vi.      The Teens Ministry: Rev. Oladeinde commended the Teens for the resilience. He added that they must not relent on their efforts. He concluded by advising them to learn from good examples around them and shun the wrong ones.

vii.      NEW MATTERS

a.      Prayer Programmes: The Senior Pastor explained that there will be more prayer programmes in the new year. He urged all department to also they put up more prayer programmes in the new year.

b.     Spiritual Focus: On spiritual, he stated that the focus in the new year will be more on:

        Soul winning and visitation which all members will be involved

        Our Home and Life will be taken seriously for all members to attend

        Workers Vigil will be compulsory for all leaders, ministers and workers

        Tuesday Bible Study will henceforth be compulsory for all workers and leaders as we need to grow in the word of God.

        The Workers Prayer on Sundays is compulsory for all workers as those who come after 7:30am will not be allowed to minister.

        He also urged that members and worshippers should see tithing as our core responsibility to God and the church as believers.

 

ELECTION INTO THE CHURCH COUNCIL

The election into the vacant positions in the church council was conducted by the appointed electoral committee headed by Bro. Mike Esukpa. The brethren standing for election were:

1.      Sis. Janet Atanda- Re- Nominated

2.      Bro. Oluranti Oluropo- Re-Nominated

3.      Bro. Olakunle Akinbowale Nominated

4.      Sis. Rosemary Irihbor- Nominated

After the votes were cast by the accredited members, the result was as follows:

1.      Sis. Janet Atanda 37 votes

2.      Bro. Oluranti Oluropo 30 votes

3.      Bro. Olakunle Akinbowale 27 votes

4.      Sis. Irihbor Rosemary- 11 votes.

The election result was announced by Bro. Mike Esukpa as Sis. Atanda and Bro. Oluranti were elected into the Church Council for second term and first term.

GENERAL COMMENTS

v On the cost of printing the AGMM report booklet, Sis. Akinbowale suggested that felicitation and advert placement on the report booklet can be introduced to raise some fund.

v Deacon Sowunmi observed that the economy of the country is also having effects on individual family and as such affecting the church income. He suggested that the church should rent out the shop on the premises and give out the space on the fence as this will help raise some fund for the church. He added that the microphones be looked into for effective production.

v Sis. Olorunda observed that members are not active in the place of prayer. She also observed that the beautification unit is not really doing much of recent.

v On the report, Bro. Olorunda pointed out that making the report soft copy that can be read on our mobile devices is a welcome idea and been a new trend that all must also adapt to. On Evangelism, he urged members to embrace evangelism as our core duty and not just leaving it for a certain set of people. He added that member should turn up for the mid -week activities.

v On the financial report, Bro. Kehinde Olagunju suggested the church look into having an accountant who will certify our financial report annually.

v Sis. Oladele said that she has been absent from the mid-week activities due to carry both the Youth and the Teenager in this same direction. She added that the youth should be encourage instead of criticizing them.

v On the late submission of reports, Bro. Gbolahan suggested that reports can be on monthly or quarterly basis as this will help when collating the annual reports. He also, suggested that the church should look into solar energy on the issue of using generator for programmes. He added that constant trainings should be in place for leaders.

v Bro. Maxson advised that proper measure should be put in place to ensure effective measure of both electrical gadgets and musical instruments in the church.

v On the Childrens camp, Sis. Kemi Onimole suggested that there should be an in-house programme within the church to replace the camping exercise.

v Sis. Akinbowale advised that there should be an assistant for the church secretary to ease the work of the secretary.

RESPONSE FROM THE SENIOR PASTOR: The Senior Pastor appreciated everyone for their contributions. He responded to all the points raised and promised that there will be positive improvements on every aspect of the church. He however, urged all workers, members and leaders to be ready to take responsibilities. He added that for the church to continue to soar high all hands must be on desk, we must all set our mind on our set goals and dont give room for laziness.

APPOINTMENT AND PRAYERS: The Senior Pastor announced the appointments into various offices for the year 2022/2023. Prayers were offered on all newly appointed leaders and the AGMM committee members.

ADJOURNMENT AND CLOSING: The motion for the adjournment of the meeting was moved by Bro. Sam Kalu and seconded by Sis. Naomi gave while the closing prayer was taken by Pst(Mrs.) Oladeinde at 4:10pm

 

Compiled and Submitted by

DCN. ZUBAIR SAMUEL

Church Secretary

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Senior Pastors Report

 

Many, O Lord my God, are thy wonderful works which thou hast done, and thy thoughts which are to us-ward: they cannot be reckoned up in order unto thee; if I would declare and speak of them, they are more that can be numbered (Psalm 40:5)

 

I welcome all in the precious name of our Lord and Saviour Jesus Christ. Our esteemed Ministers, Leaders, Deacon, Deaconess, Elders, Head of Ministries/Departments, all workers and all members and worshippers present at this years Annual General Membership Meeting (AGMM), I sincerely appreciate you all. Your support, prayers, faithfulness and dedication towards the work of the Lord is highly commendable. May the good God bless you richly in Jesus name.

I want to specially recognize, welcome and appreciate the Pastors and Council members for their maturity, tenacity and sense of purpose with which they worked with me in the administration of this church. Thanks a million times, I am indebted to you.

 

This year AGMM happens to be another epoch-making event which afford us another opportunity to present and receive reports of our stewardship for the past twelve months. This meeting calls for special appreciation to the one who has made it possible for us to once again gather together notwithstanding the challenges we have all generally had to grapple in recent times. Here He remains the Almighty God and to Him alone be all the glory in Jesus name. He has kept and watched over all of us.  Join me irrespective of what has happened to shout seven thunderous Halleluiah to the king of kings and the Lord of lords for His great doings in our midst.

Spiritual report, notable activities and achievements for the year under review are as contained in the Secretarys report. All these notable events and achievements were made possible by our Lord Jesus Christ and all of us who gave everything towards the realization of these achievements are recognized and appreciated.

For financial report please, peruse the treasurers report for details.

Bioyin church is a great church full of potentials and prospects. Our mission here in Bioyin is to build a people of power and excellence. Going forward, evangelism shall be our major focus;

we will intensify efforts to improve on our Home and life Centres and plant churches. Our goal shall be to build and grow a living church where the Holy Spirit will be in charge. We will together drive the things that made the early church alive for Christ. We will with the presence and power of the Holy Spirit of God abhor every form of politics and divisive spirit and allow the qualities and glories of winning, steadfastness in the apostle's doctrine through the preaching and teaching of the undiluted word of God with power of the Holy Spirit. Our church shall be a house of prayers and as we pray and go out to evangelize the word of God, God's power shall be demonstrated with signs and wonders following. We will encourage unity and fellowship among ourselves irrespective of our tribes. We will encourage giving and sharing of our substances with one another and to God. We will build a church where people will not only have respect for one another, but the love of God will be paramount. My sincere prayer is that God will continue to stir up our hearts and spirits for this great assignment and that none of us will fail God in Jesus name. Our basic objective will be to concentrate on consolidating on the gains of the previous years by pursuing the goals of attaining a Worship Service attendance of at least 350 regular worshipers made up of at least 200 adults and 150 children. We intend to pioneer new church, improve on the general outlook, facilities and equipment in our present building. To achieve this, we will drive to increase our total income. We will also intensify efforts in our desire to continuously strive to build a spiritually filled, excellent minded, reliable, matured and heaven bound worshipers and ensure the improved relationship between worshipers and members of the church.

 

I remain particularly grateful to the Almighty God who has been the source of strength and encouragement to us all. I am also appreciative of the tremendous support and understanding that I have continued to enjoy from my reliable companion and wife, Ibukun and my children. I appreciate all Pastors, Church Council members, leaders, members and worshipers for their sacrifice, cooperation and understanding. May the Lord continue to bless you all for your labor of love in Jesus name. I appeal to all to please follow us as we lead by the spirit of God. Offer your support by your presence in various programs and meetings, evangelism and invitation of friends and relations to the church and also ensure that you're faithful in all ramifications. I encourage you all to be focused and prayerful. Our God has made a promise, it is an everlasting

covenant, He has promised never to leave us. He will surely take us to those NEW HEIGHTs in Jesus name. Amen

 

Beloved, as I bring this report to a close, I pray that the good Lord by His mercies will continue to sustain and keep each and every one of us in Jesus name. Remember me and my family in your prayers. You are blessed. Do remain rupturable, Maranatha.

Yours in His Service 

REV. OLADEINDE O.

Senior Pasto

GOALS FOR 2023/2024

  In the New Year, soul winning will be our major priority.

  Training and workshop for all leaders and workers will be our priority.

  More Revival programmes will be put in place this year.

CONCLUSION: I appreciate all members for your love and understanding. God bless you. We hope to do better together in the Vineyard of God in this New Year.

CHRISTIAN EDUCATION DEPARTMENT

 

PREAMBLE: Our Halelluyah belongs to the Almighty God who retain us as creatures that give him pleasure as we navigated the events/programmes we set out for the church year (Rev. 4 :11)

GOALS: To use Gods word as a standard to develop members to be Christ-like indeed (spiritually and physically) through teaching; train/retrain workers and members alike on the platforms provided by the church which includes: -

a. Sunday School

b.                 Bible Study

c.                  Home Ministry

d.                 General Enlightenment sessions and seminars

ACHIEVEMENT

  All activities of the Sunday school and home ministry were duly attended to. (Check the GSS and Head of Home Ministry reports)

  Our Bible study session were vibrant where we considered many of our church doctrinal topics and gave good attention to the essence of our Christian faith with subject that touched daily Christian (practical) living and integrity in services at all human endeavours (ecclesiastical; business and secular).

  We held a seminar vigil for children workers using a professional from our Ikorodu Zone. The attendance was encouraging and many of the concerned workers were greatly impacted upon.

CHALLENGES

  Poor attendance at Sunday school previews and home fellowship stalled our plans in those directions

  Many members are still outside the Bible study net.

  Offering at all programmes of this ministry are generally poor. This slowed down our welfare efforts for workers.

RECOMMENDATION

  The ministry must step-up its drive to get more members to participate (attendance) in its programmes.

  The Training unit needs to pay good attention to the re-training of workers.

CONCLUSION/APPRECIATION: We appreciate the support of the senior Pastor and church secretary in all our activities during the church year. The Bible study coordinator (sis. Akinbowale) stood very tall in the performance of her assignment. May the lord bless all of us in Jesus name.

 

 

 

O. ADUNGBE

Christian Education Minister.

A. THE SUNDAY SCHOOL

 

PREAMBLE: From the rising of the sun unto the going down of the same the LORD'S name is to be praised (Psalms 113:3)

ACHIEVEMENTS

  There is consistence movement of Students from one class to another during the year.

  Three (3) Students were baptized with their certificate issued.

  Annual National Sunday School Promotion Week was held successfully

  Much improvement in the Sunday School attendance.

  Quarterly Sunday School rally was replaced with House Fellowship Enlistment Day in March 2023.

  There is increase in the number of Students compare to the previous year.

  There are some dedicated Teachers who gave themselves for service without being forced.

PROBLEMS/CHALLENGES

  No regular Sunday School preview during the year since the monthly worker's vigil was not regular.

  No enough time for Sunday School to preview after the day teaching

  Lateness of some Teachers and the Students to the Sunday School is a major setback.

  Poor visitation/phone calling by the Teachers to their Students.

  Time allocated to Sunday School not enough

  More committed and dedicated Teachers are needed

  Inability to have special programs for Sunday School

  Inability to have workers training and Seminar for the Teachers.

GOALS/RECOMMENDATION

  To be more committed and dedicated to the ministry

  To encourage more people to attend Sunday School in this coming year.

  To introduce encouragement packages for both Students and the Teachers

  To bring back the culture of visitation/phone calling to the Sunday School department
